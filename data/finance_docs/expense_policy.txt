CORPORATE EXPENSE POLICY

GENERAL EXPENSE GUIDELINES

Reimbursable Expenses
TechCorp reimburses legitimate business expenses incurred while performing job duties. All expenses must be:
- Ordinary and necessary for business purposes
- Reasonable in amount
- Properly documented with receipts
- Pre-approved when required
- Submitted within 60 days of incurrence

Non-Reimbursable Expenses
The following expenses will not be reimbursed:
- Personal entertainment or meals
- Alcohol (except client entertainment)
- Traffic violations and parking tickets
- Personal travel insurance
- Upgrade costs for personal convenience
- Lost or damaged personal items

TRAVEL EXPENSES

Air Travel
Corporate travel policy for flights:
- Economy class required for flights under 6 hours
- Business class allowed for international flights over 6 hours
- Book through corporate travel agency when possible
- Advance booking required (14 days minimum)
- Changes and cancellations must be reported immediately

Preferred Airlines:
- American Airlines (primary corporate contract)
- Delta Air Lines (secondary)
- Southwest Airlines (domestic short-haul)
- United Airlines (international routes)

Ground Transportation
Approved ground transportation options:
- Airport shuttles and public transportation (preferred)
- Taxi or rideshare for business necessity
- Rental cars for multi-day trips or remote locations
- Personal vehicle mileage at IRS standard rate
- Parking fees at airport and hotels

Rental Car Policy:
- Compact or mid-size vehicles only
- Decline insurance if covered by corporate policy
- Refuel before return to avoid premium charges
- No luxury or specialty vehicles without approval
- GPS rental not reimbursed (use mobile apps)

Lodging Accommodations
Hotel expense guidelines:
- Business-class accommodations appropriate
- Government/corporate rates when available
- No resort or luxury properties without approval
- Maximum rates: $200/night domestic, $300/night international
- Reasonable location relative to business purpose

Preferred Hotel Chains:
- Hilton properties (corporate discount available)
- Marriott/Bonvoy properties
- Hyatt hotels
- Embassy Suites for extended stays
- Local business hotels when rates are competitive

MEALS AND ENTERTAINMENT

Business Meals
Meal expense policies:
- Per-meal limits: Breakfast $25, Lunch $35, Dinner $75
- Daily maximum: $135 for full-day travel
- Business purpose required (meetings, client entertainment)
- Attendee names and business relationship documented
- Alcohol limited to client entertainment only

Travel Meals:
- Airport meal costs covered during travel days
- Hotel meal charges reimbursed when restaurant unavailable
- Room service acceptable for late arrivals/early departures
- Continental breakfast included in hotel rate preferred
- No reimbursement for mini-bar charges

Client Entertainment
Guidelines for entertaining clients and prospects:
- Prior approval required for events over $200
- Business purpose clearly documented
- Attendee list with titles and companies
- Reasonable and customary for industry
- Follow all applicable laws and regulations

Conference and Meeting Expenses
Professional conference attendance:
- Registration fees covered with advance approval
- Standard travel and lodging policies apply
- Networking events and sponsored meals included
- Materials and educational resources reimbursed
- Follow-up report required within 30 days

OFFICE AND EQUIPMENT EXPENSES

Office Supplies
Reimbursable office expenses:
- Basic office supplies and materials
- Business books and subscriptions
- Professional software licenses (approved list)
- Printing and shipping costs
- Office furniture with approval

Equipment Purchases
Company equipment procurement:
- All computer hardware through IT department
- Mobile devices through corporate accounts
- Software licenses through approved vendors
- Office furniture through facilities management
- Personal equipment purchases not reimbursed

Professional Services
Approved professional service expenses:
- Legal consultation for business matters
- Accounting and tax preparation services
- Professional development and training
- Industry conference registrations
- Professional association memberships

COMMUNICATION EXPENSES

Phone and Internet
Communication expense policies:
- Company-provided mobile phones for eligible employees
- Partial reimbursement for personal phone used for business
- Home internet partial reimbursement for remote workers
- International roaming charges for business travel
- Conference calling services and video subscriptions

Monthly Reimbursements:
- Mobile phone: $75 maximum for business use portion
- Home internet: $50 maximum for remote workers
- Landline: $40 maximum if required for business
- International calls: Actual business-related charges
- Data overage charges for company devices

TRANSPORTATION EXPENSES

Personal Vehicle Use
Mileage reimbursement guidelines:
- IRS standard mileage rate applied
- Business travel only (home to office not reimbursed)
- Detailed trip log required
- Reasonable route selection expected
- Tolls and parking reimbursed separately

Public Transportation
Mass transit expense policy:
- Local transportation for business meetings
- Airport transfers and city travel
- Taxi and rideshare for safety or efficiency
- Train travel for regional business trips
- Monthly transit passes for regular commuting

Parking Expenses
Parking cost reimbursements:
- Business meeting parking fees
- Airport parking for business travel
- Hotel parking charges
- Client site parking costs
- Personal commuting parking not covered

TECHNOLOGY EXPENSES

Business Software
Software and technology expenses:
- Professional software licenses and subscriptions
- Cloud service subscriptions for business use
- Training and certification programs
- Online learning platforms and courses
- Business app subscriptions with approval

Hardware and Accessories
Approved technology purchases:
- Business accessories for company equipment
- Ergonomic equipment for home office
- Presentation equipment and adapters
- External monitors and input devices
- Network and security equipment

DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

Receipt Requirements
All expense claims must include:
- Original receipt or electronic copy
- Date and amount clearly visible
- Vendor name and location
- Payment method documented
- Business purpose explanation

Electronic Receipts
Digital receipt guidelines:
- PDF or image format acceptable
- Complete transaction information visible
- Email confirmations suitable for online purchases
- Mobile app receipts from approved vendors
- Credit card statements as supporting documentation

Missing Receipt Procedures
When receipts are unavailable:
- Maximum $25 without receipt
- Signed affidavit explaining circumstances
- Detailed description of expense
- Manager approval required
- Limited to 3 occurrences per year

APPROVAL PROCESS

Expense Approval Levels
Approval requirements by amount:
- Under $100: Manager approval
- $100-$500: Department head approval
- $500-$2,000: Director approval
- Over $2,000: VP approval and advance authorization
- Foreign travel: VP approval regardless of amount

Pre-Approval Requirements
Advance approval needed for:
- All travel expenses over $1,000
- Client entertainment over $200
- Professional conference attendance
- Equipment purchases over $500
- Foreign business travel

SUBMISSION AND PAYMENT

Expense Report Deadlines
Submission requirements:
- Monthly submission by 5th of following month
- Maximum 60 days from expense date
- Quarterly submission minimum required
- Travel expenses within 30 days of return
- Late submissions require manager approval

Payment Processing
Reimbursement timeline:
- Standard processing: 10 business days
- Direct deposit to employee bank account
- Payment with next regular payroll cycle
- Wire transfers for international expenses
- Credit card payments for corporate cards

CORPORATE CREDIT CARDS

Card Usage Guidelines
Corporate card policies:
- Business expenses only
- Personal use strictly prohibited
- Prompt payment required to avoid interest
- Lost or stolen cards reported immediately
- Receipts required for all transactions

Eligible Cardholders:
- Employees with regular travel requirements
- Department managers and above
- Sales team members
- Executives and senior staff
- Project managers with client responsibilities

INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL

Foreign Expense Considerations
International business travel requirements:
- Passport and visa costs covered
- Currency exchange receipts required
- Local transportation and meal allowances
- International phone and data charges
- Travel insurance for extended trips

Per Diem Allowances
Daily allowances for international travel:
- Meals: Based on US State Department rates
- Incidental expenses: $5-10 per day
- Local transportation: Actual reasonable costs
- Gratuities: Customary amounts for services
- Emergency expenses: Case-by-case approval

VIOLATION AND ENFORCEMENT

Policy Violations
Expense policy violations include:
- Submitting false or inflated expenses
- Personal use of corporate credit cards
- Failure to submit required documentation
- Exceeding approved expense limits
- Submitting expenses after deadlines

Disciplinary Actions
Violations may result in:
- Expense claim denial
- Required repayment of improper charges
- Suspension of expense privileges
- Formal disciplinary action
- Termination for serious violations

Expense System Training
Required training covers:
- Online expense system usage

- Approval workflow processes
- Policy compliance requirements
- Mobile app functionality

Support Resources
Help available through:
- Expense system help desk
- Finance department consultation
- Manager guidance and approval
- Online policy documentation
- Training videos and guides

Review and Updates
This policy is reviewed annually and updated as needed. Changes are communicated through:
- Company-wide announcements
