PROCUREMENT POLICY

PROCUREMENT OVERVIEW

Purpose and Scope
TechCorp's procurement policy ensures efficient, transparent, and compliant purchasing processes across all departments. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and vendors involved in purchasing goods and services.

Procurement Objectives:
- Obtain best value for company investments
- Ensure compliance with legal and regulatory requirements
- Maintain ethical business practices
- Establish consistent procurement procedures
- Minimize procurement risks

PURCHASING AUTHORITY

Authorization Levels
Spending authority by role and amount:

Individual Contributors:
- Office supplies: Up to $200
- Software tools: Up to $500 with manager approval
- Training materials: Up to $300
- No capital equipment authority

Managers and Supervisors:
- Department supplies: Up to $2,000
- Software licenses: Up to $5,000
- Equipment replacement: Up to $10,000
- Professional services: Up to $7,500

Directors:
- Capital equipment: Up to $25,000
- Major software purchases: Up to $50,000
- Consultant agreements: Up to $75,000
- Multi-year contracts: Up to $100,000

Vice Presidents:
- Strategic purchases: Up to $150,000
- Major vendor contracts: Up to $250,000
- Capital projects: Up to $200,000
- International contracts: Any amount with legal review

C-Level Executives:
- Unlimited authority with board approval for amounts over $500,000
- Strategic partnerships and acquisitions
- Major capital investments
- Long-term contractual commitments

PROCUREMENT PROCESS

Purchase Request Procedures
Standard procurement workflow:
1. Complete purchase requisition form
2. Obtain necessary approvals
3. Submit to procurement team
4. Vendor selection and negotiation
5. Purchase order generation
6. Goods/services receipt verification
7. Invoice processing and payment

Required Information:
- Detailed description of goods/services
- Business justification and budget code
- Requested delivery date
- Preferred vendor (if applicable)
- Cost estimates and comparison quotes

Competitive Bidding Requirements
Mandatory competitive bidding for:
- Purchases over $25,000
- Multi-year service contracts
- Strategic supplier relationships
- Capital equipment acquisitions
- New vendor relationships

Bidding Process:
- Minimum 3 qualified vendors solicited
- Request for Proposal (RFP) development
- Vendor presentation and evaluation
- Reference checking and due diligence
- Final selection and contract negotiation

VENDOR MANAGEMENT

Vendor Selection Criteria
Evaluation factors for new vendors:
- Financial stability and business reputation
- Technical capability and experience
- Quality certifications and standards
- Competitive pricing and value proposition
- Customer service and support capabilities

Due Diligence Requirements:
- Business license and insurance verification
- Financial statements review
- Reference checks with existing customers
- Background checks for key personnel
- Compliance with industry regulations

Preferred Vendor Program
Benefits of preferred vendor status:
- Streamlined ordering processes
- Volume discount pricing
- Priority support and service
- Extended payment terms
- Exclusive product access

Vendor Performance Monitoring:
- Quarterly business reviews
- Service level agreement tracking
- Quality metrics and scorecards
- Customer satisfaction surveys
- Continuous improvement initiatives

CONTRACT MANAGEMENT

Contract Types and Terms
Standard contract categories:
- Purchase orders for one-time purchases
- Master service agreements for ongoing services
- Software licensing agreements
- Professional services contracts
- Equipment lease agreements

Contract Requirements:
- Clear scope of work and deliverables
- Defined payment terms and conditions
- Service level agreements and penalties
- Termination and renewal clauses
- Intellectual property and confidentiality terms

Contract Approval Process
Legal review required for:
- Contracts over $100,000
- Multi-year agreements
- International vendor contracts
- Software licensing with data access
- Professional services with IP creation

Risk Assessment:
- Liability and insurance requirements
- Data security and privacy compliance
- Regulatory and compliance obligations
- Business continuity and disaster recovery
- Vendor lock-in and exit strategies
